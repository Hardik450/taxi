Driver Registration Guidelines:


  1. Eligibility Criteria:
    • Drivers must possess a valid driver's license issued by the appropriate authority.
    • The vehicle must meet all legal requirements for commercial operation, including registration, insurance, and safety inspections.
    • No history of involvement in illegal activities, criminal offenses, or FIRs registered in the driver's name in the past.
  2. Registration Process:
    • Submit an online application providing personal details, contact information, and vehicle specifications.
    • Upload relevant documents, including driver's license, vehicle registration, insurance, and other required certifications.
    • Consent to background checks and verification procedures as part of the registration process, including criminal record checks and FIR history verification.
  3. Vehicle Requirements:
    • Vehicles must be well-maintained, clean, and in good working condition.
    • Ensure vehicles meet company standards for safety, cleanliness, and comfort.
    • Regularly service vehicles to maintain optimal performance and passenger safety.
  4. Professional Conduct:
    • Maintain a professional demeanor at all times when interacting with passengers and company representatives.
    • Follow all traffic laws and regulations while operating the vehicle.
    • Provide a safe and comfortable ride experience for passengers, prioritizing their well-being and satisfaction.
  5. Customer Service:
    • Be courteous, respectful, and attentive to passengers' needs and preferences.
    • Communicate effectively with passengers to ensure a positive and enjoyable ride experience.
    • Handle passenger inquiries, complaints, and feedback promptly and professionally.
  6. Safety and Security:
    • Ensure passenger safety by adhering to speed limits, using seat belts, and following safe driving practices.
    • Implement security measures to protect passengers and their belongings during the ride.
    • Report any incidents, accidents, or safety concerns to the company immediately.
  7. Compliance and Regulations:
    • Stay informed about local transportation laws, regulations, and industry standards.
    • Comply with company policies, procedures, and guidelines for driver conduct and vehicle operation.
    • Maintain necessary permits, licenses, and certifications required for commercial driving.
  8. Continuous Improvement:
    • Participate in training programs and workshops provided by the company to enhance driving skills, customer service, and safety knowledge.
    • Seek feedback from passengers and company management to identify areas for improvement and implement necessary changes.
  9. Zero Tolerance Policy:
    • Uphold a zero-tolerance policy towards discrimination, harassment, substance abuse, or any other behavior that violates company policies or compromises passenger safety.
By adhering to these guidelines, drivers can ensure compliance with legal requirements, maintain a positive reputation, and provide safe and reliable transportation services to passengers while representing the taxi providing company.
